# Article model

## Content types

Articles contain three types of content:

- **Explanation** — describes what something is, how it works, or why it matters
- **Instructions** — step-by-step procedural guidance
- **Reference** — look-up content such as field definitions, option lists, or tables

## Article types

These are guidelines, not strict rules. Real articles may blend content types or deviate from the typical pattern.

Article types apply recursively. A feature may have its own setup article; sub-features may have setup articles too. The type describes the article's role relative to its subject, regardless of where that subject sits in the product hierarchy.

| Type | Purpose | Primary content | Secondary content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overview | High-level orientation | Explanation | — |
| Setup | One-off upfront configuration | Explanation, Instructions | Reference |
| Feature | Repeated day-to-day use | Explanation, Instructions | Reference |
| Reference | Look-up | Reference | — |

## Standard sections by product

- All **Omni** articles include an **Overview** section.
- All **Core** articles include a **Prerequisites** section.

## Composition within a subtopic

Articles live within subtopics. A well-formed subtopic typically contains:

- **One** overview article (the main orientation for the subtopic)
- **One or a small number** of setup articles (covering initial configuration)
- **The bulk** of articles as feature articles (covering day-to-day use)
- **Some** reference articles as needed
